Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular Indian street food dish made with a spicy and flavorful vegetable curry (Bhaji) served with soft bread rolls (Pav). It's a favorite among many, and you can easily make it at home. Here's a simple Pav Bhaji recipe for you:

Ingredients:

  • For the Bhaji:
    • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as potatoes, peas, carrots, bell peppers, and cauliflower), finely chopped
    • 2 large onions, finely chopped
    • 2 large tomatoes, finely chopped
    • 1/2 cup green bell pepper (capsicum), finely chopped
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
    • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
    • 2-3 tablespoons Pav Bhaji masala (readily available in stores)
    • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to your spice level)
    • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
    • salt to taste
    • 4 tablespoons butter or oil
    • Chopped fresh coriander leaves for garnish
    • Lemon wedges for serving

  • For the Pav (Bread Rolls):
    • 8 soft pav (bread rolls)
    • 2 tablespoons butter

Instructions:

  1. Boil the mixed vegetables until they are soft and tender. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pan, heat 2 tablespoons of butter or oil. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic, grated ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add the chopped green bell pepper (capsicum) and sauté for a few minutes until it becomes tender.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
  6. ow, add the Pav Bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for a few minutes until the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  7. Mash the boiled vegetables and add them to the pan. Mix everything together, and let it cook for a few minutes.
  8. If the bhaji is too dry, you can add a little water to get the desired consistency. Simmer for about 10-15 minutes, mashing the vegetables as you go along.
  9. In a separate pan, slit the pav rolls and toast them with a little butter until they are lightly browned and crisp.
  10. Serve the hot Bhaji with the toasted Pav. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and a wedge of lemon. You can also serve it with some chopped onions and extra butter on the side.

Enjoy your homemade Pav Bhaji! It's a delicious and satisfying meal, perfect for any time of the day.
